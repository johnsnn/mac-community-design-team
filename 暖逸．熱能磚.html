暖逸．熱能磚 (HydroFence)

By Cherry, Krystal & Skyla

About the Project

In Macau, a densely populated city with over 14% of its population aged 65+, frequent rainfall (2000 mm annually) and occasional winter frost cause slippery surfaces, leading to over 35% of elderly accidents yearly. These slips result in high costs (MOP 120,000 per hip fracture patient) and a 20% disability rate. The "Thermal Dehydration System" transforms traditional anti-slip tiles by actively removing water and melting ice, enhancing ground safety and supporting Macau’s goal to be an "aging-friendly city" through innovative infrastructure.

The "Thermal Dehydration System" enhances anti-slip tiles by actively removing water and ice, improving safety and supporting Macau’s "aging-friendly city" goal.
When there is water or ice on the floor, the moisture sensor within the floor tiles automatically detects it and activates warm steam to dry it, faster than sunlight.
The surface remains comfortable for barefoot walking seasonally: in summer, it evaporates water at ~40°C; in winter, it raises to ~50°C to melt ice and dry surfaces, countering higher slip risks from cold weather.
The surface has hidden anti-slip patterns, so it reduces the risk of slipping by 80% and prevents disabilities and illnesses resulting from falls.
After a certain amount of water is collected, rainwater will flow from the drainage pipes of the paving stones into the park's own large drainage system, speeding up drainage during heavy rainfall.
